Gordon Brown has outlined what he thinks should be the key themes of New Labour's next general election bid.Mr Blair said a decision had yet to be taken over how the election would be run but the chancellor's role would be "central".Mr Brown argued that under New Labour the country had changed for the better and that was "in part" because of Mr Brown's management of the economy.Writing in the Guardian newspaper, Mr Brown outlined his view of the direction New Labour should be taking.But the prime minister insisted he did not know what date the poll would take place despite speculation about 5 May.
