Oil prices recovered in Asian trade on Tuesday, after falling in New York on milder winter weather across the US.With winter temperatures staying relatively high in the northern US, a barrel of light crude ended Monday down $1.33 to $42.12.Despite a fall of more than $12 a barrel from the record highs reached in late October, the price of crude oil remains almost 30% higher than year-ago levels."The weather has been the Achilles' heel of this market," said ABN AMRO analyst John Brady.
